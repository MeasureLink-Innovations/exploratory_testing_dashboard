## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are **QA engineers in industrial/lab environments**. They operate in a dual-mode workflow: reflective and structured during planning/debriefing, and fast, execution-focused during active testing. The interface should support rapid evidence capture without sacrificing clarity.

### Brand Personality
**Light, Precise, Reliable.** The product should feel like a high-end technical instrument: trustworthy, exact, and transparent in operation.

### Aesthetic Direction
**E-Ink / Paper Style.** High contrast, intentional whitespace, and strong legibility.
- **Visual Direction**: Brutalist-adjacent but refined; strict grid alignment; utilitarian tables and metadata presentation.
- **Emotional Tone**: **Mostly calm + confident**.
- **Anti-References**: Avoid "cockpit" overload (too many simultaneous metrics, controls, and alerts).

### Design Principles
1. **Zen Execution**: During active sessions, minimize non-essential UI to reduce cognitive load.
2. **Alignment as Information**: Use consistent spacing and mathematical alignment to communicate precision and reliability.
3. **Paper-Thin Layers**: Prefer borders, line weight, and subtle fills over heavy depth effects.
4. **Persistent Simplicity**: Keep controls low-friction and direct (e.g., compact toggles/sliders over complex control groups).
5. **Calm Operational Confidence**: Prioritize predictable interactions and stable visual hierarchy so users feel in control in industrial/lab contexts.
